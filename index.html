How to Roll With It

  1. Add the Calendar First: Click the '+ Google Calendar' button at the bottom right of the calendar to add it to your Google Calendar. This lets you create and manage interview slots. One click, and you're in the cool kids' club.
  2. Add Your Time, Be a Star: Got a free hour to help someone? Pop an event on the calendar. Call it "Mock Interview - [Your Name/Skill]" (like "Mock Interview - PM Prep with Saurao"). In the description, add "Ping me on LinkedIn!" with your LinkedIn link. Now everyone knows you're a big deal.
  3. Pick a Time, Peek Their Profile: Look at the calendar like it’s a menu. See a slot you like? Click the LinkedIn link in the description to check out who they are (networking bonus!). Message them on LinkedIn with this:
    Hi [Name], I’m [Your Name]. Can I grab your [time] slot on [date] from Connect? I’m practicing for [skill/job]. Thanks!
    Hit copy, paste it, and you’re halfway to a new work buddy.
  4. Make It Official: If you’re the star, chat on LinkedIn, then add their email to your event as a guest. Hit save. Google Calendar sends them a fancy invite with a Meet link. Look at you, all professional and stuff.
  5. Meet Up: Click the Meet link when it’s time. Talk, practice, win. Done! Don’t sleep through it, or LinkedIn will tell tales.

Pro Moves for the Squad

  • Recurring Vibes: Too busy to keep adding slots? Set it to repeat—click “Does not repeat” and pick “Every Wednesday” or whatever. Auto-pilot mode, activated.
  • Time Zone Check: Slap your time zone in the description (e.g., “10 AM IST”) so no one’s ghosting you from another planet.
  • Chat It Up: Tick “Guests can see guest list” in event settings. Let peeps comment “Yo, I’m in!” instead of LinkedIn-ing. Less typing, more winning.
  • Clean the Slate: Ditch a slot? Delete it. No one likes a calendar full of fake promises.
